Topic Coverage

  • Green chips = covered (1–2 posts). Amber = saturated (3+).
  • Aim new briefs at gaps — keywords with 0 posts.
  • New posts on saturated tags barely move SEO. Diminishing returns.
  • Filter by brand to spot gaps in one brand the other has covered.

Draft Queue

  • Approve sends straight to publish. Be sure first.
  • Amend opens an inline editor — fix title, summary, body.
  • Reject with specific feedback — it's fed to next week's runs as "don't repeat this".
  • Watch for AI tells: "it's worth noting", "in conclusion", em-dashes everywhere. Amend before approving.

Add Content

  • Notes → saved as briefs, steers the next generation run.
  • Paste → article text becomes a source seed for future posts.
  • Upload → PDF or photo of magazine. Claude extracts the editorial content.
  • You can also send URLs / photos / PDFs to the Telegram bot from your phone.

SEO leverage

  • The engine now does SERP analysis automatically — it sees what already ranks for the keyword and writes into the gap.
  • Best title format: specific + searchable, not clickbait.
  • 800–1500 words is the sweet spot.
  • Aim for 2–3 internal links per post (the engine handles this).

Cadence

  • Review 2× per week — Mon + Thu is a good rhythm.
  • Aim for 2–3 published posts per brand per week.
  • Each rejected post compounds quality — be specific in feedback.
  • Reels: see /social — edit copy and re-render videos.

What NOT to do

  • Don't approve below 8/10 evaluation score without amending first.
  • Don't write briefs that just rephrase a saturated topic.
  • Don't reject without feedback — that's a wasted signal.
  • Don't approve fabricated stats or first-person claims. Amend or reject.
